## Post 3 — The architecture flex (Day 4)

**Best as:** 1-image post (screenshot of the audit-trail page with the consent ID column visible).

> Most credit bureaus log "user X pulled report Y at time Z."
>
> We log: which consent ID authorised it, what purpose was declared, which records were touched, and we anchor the hash to a public blockchain every six hours so nothing can be silently rewritten.
>
> Why? Because if you're going to bridge three sensitive datasets — credit, collateral, fiscal — the consumer has to be able to prove what happened to her data.
>
> Consent isn't a checkbox. It's the architecture.
>
> #PrivacyByDesign #FinTechArchitecture

## Voice & tone notes

- **Specific over generic.** "She had a stack of paper files" beats "many lenders struggle."
- **Numbers earn trust.** Use real ones. If you don't have a stat, don't invent one — use an architecture fact instead ("100% audit-logged" is true and powerful).
- **Confident, not boastful.** "We built X" not "We're proud to announce X."
- **Short paragraphs.** LinkedIn truncates after ~3 lines on mobile. Front-load the hook.
- **One CTA per post.** Don't ask for likes, comments, shares, follows, and a demo all at once.
- **Reply to every comment** for the first 48 hours after each post — that's how the algorithm rewards you.
