# Ghana Credit Registry — Data Protection and Privacy Policy

**Systems In Motion Limited**
**Version 1.0 | Aligned with Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843)**

---

## 1. Introduction

This Data Protection and Privacy Policy governs the collection, processing, storage, and sharing of personal data within the Ghana Credit Registry operated by Systems In Motion Limited. This policy ensures compliance with the Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843) and the Credit Reporting Act, 2007 (Act 726).

## 5. Data Subject Rights

### 5.1 Rights Under Act 843

| Right | Implementation | Response Time |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Right of access | Self-service portal or written request | 30 days |
| Right to rectification | Dispute management system | 2-5 business days |
| Right to erasure | After retention period expires | Automated |
| Right to restrict processing | Consent revocation | Immediate |
| Right to data portability | Credit report export (PDF) | Same day |
| Right to object | Written objection to DPO | 14 days |
| Right to be informed | Privacy notice at data collection | At point of collection |

### 5.2 Annual Free Credit Report
Per Act 726, Section 15, every individual is entitled to one free credit report per calendar year from each licensed credit bureau.

## 6. Data Security Measures

### 6.1 Technical Safeguards

| Measure | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| Encryption at rest | AES-256 for all stored personal data |
| Encryption in transit | TLS 1.2+ for all data transmission |
| Access control | Role-based access with least privilege |
| Authentication | Session-based with MFA support |
| Audit logging | SHA-256 hash chain, 10-year retention |
| Backup | Daily encrypted backups, 30-day retention |
| Intrusion detection | Application-level monitoring |

### 6.2 Organizational Safeguards

| Measure | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| Staff training | Annual data protection training |
| Confidentiality agreements | Required for all personnel |
| Access reviews | Quarterly access privilege reviews |
| Incident response plan | Documented and tested annually |
| Data classification | All data classified and labeled |
| Vendor management | DPA required for all processors |

## 7. Data Sharing and Cross-Border Transfers

### 7.1 Authorized Data Recipients

| Recipient | Purpose | Legal Basis |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Licensed credit institutions | Credit assessment | Act 726, Section 11 |
| Bank of Ghana | Regulatory oversight | Act 726, Section 5 |
| Data Protection Commission | Compliance verification | Act 843, Section 51 |
| Data subjects | Self-access | Act 726, Section 14 |
| Courts | Legal proceedings | Court order |

### 7.2 Cross-Border Data Transfer Rules
Per Act 843, Section 37, personal data may only be transferred outside Ghana if:
- The recipient country provides adequate data protection
- Explicit consent of the data subject is obtained
- Transfer is necessary for contract performance
- Bank of Ghana authorization for regulatory data

## 9. Data Retention and Disposal

### 9.1 Retention Schedule

| Data Category | Retention Period | Disposal Method |
|--------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Active credit data | Duration of facility + 7 years | Secure deletion |
| Closed accounts | 7 years from closure | Secure deletion |
| Court judgments | 5 years from judgment or resolution | Secure deletion |
| Dishonoured cheques | 3 years from occurrence | Secure deletion |
| Audit logs | 10 years | Archive then deletion |
| Consent records | Duration of consent + 7 years | Secure deletion |
| Dispute records | 7 years from resolution | Secure deletion |

### 9.2 Automated Enforcement
The system implements automated retention policy enforcement:
- Daily scan for expired records
- Automated archival for records approaching retention limit
- Secure deletion with audit trail of disposal
- BoG notification before bulk data disposal
