## 1. Executive Summary

Africa Credit Hub presents its Cross-Jurisdictional Central Data Hub (CDH) v2.5 as a turnkey credit bureau and credit registry solution for the Republic of Liberia. Our platform is purpose-built for the African financial ecosystem and is already operational in Ghana under Bank of Ghana CRB v1.1 compliance standards.

Liberia's financial sector is at a critical inflection point. The Central Bank of Liberia (CBL) has established the regulatory foundation for credit reporting through CBL Credit Reference Bureau Regulations (CBL/SD/003/2010), and the growing demand for financial inclusion requires a robust, modern credit information system that serves banks, microfinance institutions, mobile money operators, and the CBL itself.

Our solution offers:

- A fully operational, production-proven credit bureau platform
- Pre-built compliance with ECOWAS and African Union credit reporting standards
- Support for the Liberian Dollar (LRD) alongside multi-currency capabilities
- Scalable SaaS architecture that can be deployed within 8-12 weeks
- Cross-border entity resolution for Liberian nationals with credit histories in neighboring ECOWAS states (Sierra Leone, Guinea, Cote d'Ivoire)

## 2. The Liberian Credit Landscape

### 2.1 Current State

| Indicator | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Credit Registry Operator | Central Bank of Liberia (CBL) |
| Regulatory Framework | CBL Credit Reference Bureau Regulations (CBL/SD/003/2010) |
| Data Protection Law | No specific enacted law (draft stage) |
| Credit Bureau Coverage | Approximately 2.3% of adult population |
| Banking Penetration | ~20% of adult population |
| Active Commercial Banks | 9 licensed banks |
| Microfinance Institutions | 20+ licensed MFIs |
| Mobile Money Operators | 3 active operators (Lonestar MTN, Orange Money, Africell Money) |
| Currency | Liberian Dollar (LRD) |
| Regional Memberships | ECOWAS, African Union (AU), Mano River Union |

### 2.2 Key Challenges

1. **Limited Credit Information Sharing**: Most institutions operate with siloed borrower data, leading to information asymmetry and elevated default rates.
2. **Manual Processes**: Existing credit assessment relies heavily on paper-based documentation and manual verification.
3. **Financial Inclusion Gap**: Without credit histories, ~80% of the adult population cannot access formal credit products.
4. **Cross-Border Visibility**: Significant cross-border economic activity with Sierra Leone, Guinea, and Cote d'Ivoire lacks credit information sharing mechanisms.
5. **Mobile Money Growth**: Rapid expansion of mobile financial services requires integration of digital credit data into the formal credit reporting system.

### 2.3 Opportunity

The World Bank's Doing Business indicators highlight credit information systems as a critical enabler of private sector growth. Implementing a comprehensive credit bureau solution in Liberia will:

- Reduce non-performing loan (NPL) ratios across the banking sector
- Enable risk-based lending decisions for banks and MFIs
- Expand access to credit for MSMEs and individuals
- Support CBL supervisory objectives
- Attract foreign direct investment through improved financial infrastructure transparency

## 8. Credit Scoring Framework

### 8.1 Scoring Model

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Score Range | 300 - 850 |
| Model Type | Algorithmic Behavioural Scorecard |
| Update Frequency | Real-time (recalculated on each report generation) |

### 8.2 Scoring Variables

| Variable | Weight / Impact | Description |
|----------|----------------|-------------|
| Base Score | +300 | Starting score for all borrowers |
| On-Time Payment Ratio | +500 (max) | Proportion of current/closed accounts vs total |
| Delinquent Accounts | -50 each | Accounts in delinquent or default status |
| Written-Off Accounts | -75 each | Accounts written off as losses |
| Restructured Accounts | -20 each | Restructured or rescheduled facilities |
| Active Court Judgments | -40 each | Unresolved legal judgments |
| Credit Inquiries | -5 each (max -100) | Number of recent inquiries, capped at 20 |

### 8.3 Model Validation

| Metric | Description | Current Value |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Gini Coefficient | Model discriminatory power | 0.62 |
| KS Statistic | Separation between good and bad borrowers | 0.48 |
| Rank Ordering | Risk ranking accuracy | Validated |
| Stress Testing | Model robustness under economic stress | Passed |
| Probability of Default | Estimated default probability | Calculated per account |

### 8.4 Score Reason Codes

Each credit score is accompanied by transparent reason codes explaining the key factors:

- Delinquent accounts on file
- Written-off accounts present
- Restructured loan agreements
- High number of credit inquiries
- Elevated total debt level
- Court judgments or liens on record
- Politically exposed person (PEP)
- Strong repayment track record
- Excellent payment consistency
- Limited credit history on file
